How I would approach ML at Bold

1. Understand members, programs, and outcomes
  • Work with the data and clinical teams to define who our members are, how programs are structured, and which outcomes truly matter (adherence, pain scores, balance, independence).
  • Explore historical data to identify patterns of success and drop‑off by segment.
2. Build & iterate on recommendation systems
  • Start with simple, interpretable models (e.g., heuristic + embeddings) that match members to programs based on conditions, goals, and preferences.
  • Gradually introduce collaborative filtering and sequence‑aware models (e.g., Transformer‑based approaches) to capture evolving engagement patterns.
3. Own experiments, deployment & monitoring
  • Define clear offline metrics (precision, recall for completion/engagement) and online metrics (retention, adherence, improvement scores).
  • Collaborate with engineering to expose models via robust APIs, with logging and monitoring for drift and failure cases.
4. Keep models clinically aligned & explainable
  • Work with clinicians and product to make sure recommendations are safe, appropriate, and easy to understand for members.
  • Build simple “why this recommendation” traces that help users and coaches trust the system.
5. Grow with the team
  • Help shape ML best practices at Bold: code quality, experiment tracking, documentation, and sharing learnings.
  • Stay curious about new recommendation and Generative AI approaches that could unlock better, more engaging experiences for older adults.
